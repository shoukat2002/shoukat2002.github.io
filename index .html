01
Remote Work & Wage Growth: A Post-COVID Econometric Analysis
ECON 392W  ·  R  ·  Difference-in-Differences  ·  BLS OEWS Data
Built an original econometric study quantifying the causal impact of remote work eligibility on wage trajectories across U.S. metro areas. Applied a difference-in-differences framework to Bureau of Labor Statistics OEWS data — the same analytical approach used by consulting firms to evaluate policy and market shifts. Analyzed 65,000+ annual mean wage records, controlling for occupation and metro-area fixed effects to isolate the treatment effect of remote eligibility on post-COVID wage growth.
REconometricsDiff-in-DifferencesBLS OEWSFixed EffectsCausal InferenceStatistical Modeling
// Key findings
↗Identified a statistically significant wage premium of ~8–12% for remote-eligible occupations post-COVID
↗Model explained ~35% of wage variance after controlling for metro-area and occupation fixed effects
↗Findings applicable to compensation benchmarking and labor market strategy for firms across 50+ metro areas

Returns to Education in NYC Labor Markets
Independent  ·  Python & R  ·  ACS PUMS
Investigated the education wage premium across NYC boroughs (12,000+ records, 5 industries). Applied Mincer earnings equations with borough fixed effects. Found an 18–24% premium for bachelor's holders — widest in finance, narrowest in retail.
